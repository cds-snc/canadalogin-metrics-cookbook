Call Centre

Metrics in this section are calculated from call centre activity and topic data.

Call centre metrics are useful for understanding support demand, service responsiveness, caller wait times, average call duration, and common reasons for contact. The source data is weekly, so these metrics are typically reported at a weekly level or aggregated across weeks.

The call centre tables analysts work with are:

  • call_centre.weekly_activity_report - weekly call volume and service-level summary
  • call_centre.weekly_topic_dump - individual call records with topic classifications

Activity Metrics

Metrics in this section describe call centre operations, sourced from call_centre.weekly_activity_report.

Calls Accepted

Source: call_centre.weekly_activity_report

The number of calls accepted by the call centre during a reporting period.

This metric is useful for tracking call centre demand and understanding how contact volume changes over time.

Alternative names:

  • calls received
  • accepted calls
  • weekly call volume
  • call centre demand
  • number of calls handled by the queue

Calculation. Use calls_accepted by reporting period, or sum across periods for a total.

By week:

SELECT
    date_range_start,
    date_range_end,
    CAST(calls_accepted AS BIGINT) AS calls_accepted
FROM call_centre.weekly_activity_report
ORDER BY date_range_start;

Total over a period:

SELECT
    SUM(CAST(calls_accepted AS BIGINT)) AS total_calls_accepted
FROM call_centre.weekly_activity_report
WHERE date_range_start BETWEEN DATE '2026-01-01' AND DATE '2026-05-31';

Interpretation. Calls accepted may not be identical to calls answered, depending on call centre definitions and routing logic.

Calls Answered

Source: call_centre.weekly_activity_report

The number of calls answered by call centre agents during a reporting period.

This metric is also used as a weighting variable when calculating weighted average call length and weighted average delay.

Alternative names:

  • answered calls
  • calls handled
  • agent-answered calls
  • completed calls
  • calls taken by agents

Calculation. Use calls_answered by reporting period, or sum across periods for a total.

By week:

SELECT
    date_range_start,
    date_range_end,
    CAST(calls_answered AS BIGINT) AS calls_answered
FROM call_centre.weekly_activity_report
ORDER BY date_range_start;

Total over a period:

SELECT
    SUM(CAST(calls_answered AS BIGINT)) AS total_calls_answered
FROM call_centre.weekly_activity_report
WHERE date_range_start BETWEEN DATE '2026-01-01' AND DATE '2026-05-31';

Interpretation. Calls answered is commonly used as the weighting field when combining weekly average call length or delay values.

Average Call Length

Source: call_centre.weekly_activity_report (avg_call_length_seconds, calls_answered)

The average length of answered calls.

The overall average call length is calculated as a weighted mean of avg_call_length_seconds, weighted by calls_answered. This is preferable to a simple average of weekly averages because weeks with more answered calls should contribute more to the overall result.

Alternative names:

  • average call duration
  • average handle time
  • average length of call
  • typical call length
  • average time on call

Calculation. Calculate a weighted average of weekly average call length, weighted by the number of answered calls.

Weighted average over a period:

SELECT
    SUM(
        CAST(avg_call_length_seconds AS DOUBLE)
        * CAST(calls_answered AS DOUBLE)
    )
    / NULLIF(SUM(CAST(calls_answered AS DOUBLE)), 0) AS weighted_avg_call_length_seconds
FROM call_centre.weekly_activity_report
WHERE date_range_start BETWEEN DATE '2026-01-01' AND DATE '2026-05-31';

By week:

SELECT
    date_range_start,
    date_range_end,
    CAST(avg_call_length_seconds AS DOUBLE) AS avg_call_length_seconds,
    CAST(calls_answered AS BIGINT) AS calls_answered
FROM call_centre.weekly_activity_report
ORDER BY date_range_start;

Interpretation. Use a weighted average rather than a simple average of weekly averages. A simple average may give too much influence to low-volume weeks.

Average Delay

Source: call_centre.weekly_activity_report (avg_delay_seconds, calls_answered)

The average delay before calls are answered.

This metric is useful for understanding caller wait times and call centre responsiveness. Like average call length, it should be calculated as a weighted mean to avoid low-volume weeks distorting the result.

Alternative names:

  • average wait time
  • average delay before answer
  • caller wait time
  • time to answer
  • average queue delay

Calculation. Calculate a weighted average of weekly average delay, weighted by the number of answered calls.

Weighted average over a period:

SELECT
    SUM(
        CAST(avg_delay_seconds AS DOUBLE)
        * CAST(calls_answered AS DOUBLE)
    )
    / NULLIF(SUM(CAST(calls_answered AS DOUBLE)), 0) AS weighted_avg_delay_seconds
FROM call_centre.weekly_activity_report
WHERE date_range_start BETWEEN DATE '2026-01-01' AND DATE '2026-05-31';

By week:

SELECT
    date_range_start,
    date_range_end,
    CAST(avg_delay_seconds AS DOUBLE) AS avg_delay_seconds,
    CAST(calls_answered AS BIGINT) AS calls_answered
FROM call_centre.weekly_activity_report
ORDER BY date_range_start;

Interpretation. Average delay should be interpreted alongside call volume. A low-volume week can produce unusual wait times that should not dominate the overall result.

Weekly Activity Summary

Source: call_centre.weekly_activity_report

A weekly summary of call centre activity combining volume and service-level fields. Useful for dashboards and trend reporting, or as a base for the weighted average calculations above.

Alternative names:

  • weekly call centre summary
  • weekly call activity
  • call centre weekly report
  • weekly service volume
  • weekly caller demand

Calculation. Select the key weekly reporting fields from weekly_activity_report.

SELECT
    date_range_start,
    date_range_end,
    CAST(calls_accepted AS BIGINT) AS calls_accepted,
    CAST(calls_answered AS BIGINT) AS calls_answered,
    CAST(avg_call_length_seconds AS DOUBLE) AS avg_call_length_seconds,
    CAST(avg_delay_seconds AS DOUBLE) AS avg_delay_seconds
FROM call_centre.weekly_activity_report
ORDER BY date_range_start;

Interpretation. Higher-level metrics derived from this table (averages, totals) should be clearly defined to avoid mixing counts and averages.

Topic Metrics

Metrics in this section describe why callers contact the call centre, sourced from call_centre.weekly_topic_dump.

Call Topic Count

Source: call_centre.weekly_topic_dump

The number of call records associated with each call topic.

The field statobject is cleaned by trimming and squishing whitespace before counting. This metric helps answer: “What are people calling about?”

Alternative names:

  • call reasons
  • call topics
  • reasons for calling
  • top caller issues
  • contact drivers

Calculation. Clean the statobject field by trimming whitespace and collapsing repeated whitespace to a single space, then count records by cleaned topic.

SELECT
    REGEXP_REPLACE(TRIM(statobject), '\s+', ' ') AS call_topic,
    COUNT(*) AS topic_count
FROM call_centre.weekly_topic_dump
GROUP BY REGEXP_REPLACE(TRIM(statobject), '\s+', ' ')
ORDER BY topic_count DESC;

Interpretation. Call topic counts depend on how topics are coded in the source system. Topic naming, duplicates, and inconsistent spelling can affect results.

Call Topic Share

Source: call_centre.weekly_topic_dump

The percentage of all topic records represented by each call topic.

This is useful for understanding the relative importance of each call driver compared to the overall volume.

Alternative names:

  • share of calls by topic
  • percentage of calls by reason
  • topic distribution
  • call reason mix
  • contact driver share

Calculation. Count records by cleaned topic, then divide each topic count by the total count across all topics.

WITH topic_counts AS (
    SELECT
        REGEXP_REPLACE(TRIM(statobject), '\s+', ' ') AS call_topic,
        COUNT(*) AS topic_count
    FROM call_centre.weekly_topic_dump
    GROUP BY REGEXP_REPLACE(TRIM(statobject), '\s+', ' ')
)

SELECT
    call_topic,
    topic_count,
    CAST(topic_count AS DOUBLE)
        / NULLIF(SUM(topic_count) OVER (), 0) AS topic_share
FROM topic_counts
ORDER BY topic_count DESC;

Interpretation. Topic share is useful for comparing relative demand across contact reasons. Interpret alongside total call volume.

Top 10 Call Topics

Source: call_centre.weekly_topic_dump

The ten most common call topics in the call centre topic data.

This is useful for quickly identifying the most common reasons people contact the call centre.

Alternative names:

  • top 10 call reasons
  • most common call topics
  • top contact drivers
  • main reasons for calling
  • top caller issues

Calculation. Count records by cleaned topic, calculate topic share, and return the top 10 by topic count.

WITH topic_counts AS (
    SELECT
        REGEXP_REPLACE(TRIM(statobject), '\s+', ' ') AS call_topic,
        COUNT(*) AS topic_count
    FROM call_centre.weekly_topic_dump
    GROUP BY REGEXP_REPLACE(TRIM(statobject), '\s+', ' ')
),

topic_shares AS (
    SELECT
        call_topic,
        topic_count,
        CAST(topic_count AS DOUBLE)
            / NULLIF(SUM(topic_count) OVER (), 0) AS topic_share
    FROM topic_counts
)

SELECT
    call_topic,
    topic_count,
    topic_share
FROM topic_shares
ORDER BY topic_count DESC
LIMIT 10;

Interpretation. The top 10 view is useful for quick reporting but may hide important lower-volume issues. For diagnostic work, review the full topic distribution.
